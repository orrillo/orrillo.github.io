Econometrics & Mathematical Theory

Linear and Non-linear Processes in Macroeconometrics with Marcelo J. Moreira (FGV EPGE)

Completes the characterization of linear processes under the Mallows metric, proving their limits are infinitely divisible with compound Poisson representations. It identifies conditions for symmetry and monotonicity to explain non-ergodic regime behavior, establishing that standard inference is inconsistent at the boundary where linear approximations break down.

When Enforcement Is Not Neutral: Collateral vs. Penalties with Jaime Orrillo Presented at SAET 2026 (IMPA), Session: Developments in Macro Finance

We study a dynamic credit economy with heterogeneous agents, endogenous default, and imperfect enforcement. We prove that there is no utility-penalty representation that replicates the equilibrium allocation generated by collateral constraints when penalties are bounded and wealth is heterogeneous.

Financial Economics

Hyperopic Asset Pricing: Stability and Duality in the Tail with Peter McAdam (European Central Bank) and Jaime Orrillo Working Paper

We develop a framework in which prices are determined by long-run behavior rather than local fluctuations. Our main result establishes a hyperopic version of the Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing, showing that absence of asymptotic arbitrage is equivalent to the existence of a consistent pricing functional.
